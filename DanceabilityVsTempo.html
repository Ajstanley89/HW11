Danceability vs Tempo

Have We Reached Peak Dance?

Danceability was one of the biggest factors that contributed to a song's popularity, but what makes a song danceable? Tempo surely has something to do with it. Make a song too slow, and it feels like a drag, but make a song too fast, and people can't keep up.

For each genre, there seemed to be a parabolic relationship between danceabilty and tempo. The optimum tempo for dancing was around 110 beats per minute. Furthermore, country music was the least danceable, while rap was the most danceable. Maybe Country would be more popular if more people could dance to it.
